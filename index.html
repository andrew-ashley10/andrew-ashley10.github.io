Modeling Disease Spread Through Population

The hypothesis in this project studies a simulated spread of disease. This disease spread occurs through a population until the either a vulnerable population has been infected, or the whole population has been infected; this limit depends on the test that is being conducted. The disease spread occurs in two different ways depending on the test, either through direct contact or airborne spread. In the direct contact spread each when an uninfected walker is next to an infected walker, there is a chance of a spread depending on vaccination of the uninfected walker. The airborne spread occurs when an uninfected walker is near but not necessarily next to the infected walker.
